Audit organization activity

View settings activity

Learn more …


Try it live on the next page.

The organization Activity page shows a history of updates to configuration settings for the selected organization, such as activity related to:

  • Organization settings, including management of business units, authentication configuration, email notifications, and general settings for documents
  • Management of groups and group membership, users and invitations, permissions and roles
  • Inventory record settings, including management of inventory record types and record fields
  • Artifact settings, including management of artifact types, severities, and fields
  • Document settings, including management of document types, templates, and library blocks
  • Governance settings, including management of regulations or polices and related assessments, as well as risk areas and validation guidelines
  • Workflow settings, including workflow creations, publications, and deletions
  • Integration settings, including integration setup and secret management
  • Webhook settings, including webhook secret management

To review settings activity for your organization:

  1. In the left sidebar, click Activity.
  2. Select the Settings Activity tab:
  • Narrow down settings activity by clicking on the tab for the category you want to view activity for. When enough categories are displayed, click or to scroll through left or right respectively through the list.
  • View more details about specific activity by clicking when the option is available on an entry.

Review settings activity

  2. Select the Settings Activity tab:
  • Narrow down settings activity by clicking on the tab for the category you want to view activity for.
  • View more details about specific activity by clicking when the option is available on an entry.

View record activity

The record Activity page shows a history of activities for the selected inventory record, including actions performed by users in your organization, activity from automated workflows, and updates generated via the ValidMind Library, such as:

  • Fields updated
  • Stage transitions
  • Updates to documents: documentation, validation reports, ongoing monitoring reports, or custom documents
  • Test results or metrics added via the library
  • Artifacts added, updated, or removed
  • User comment creation and replies on documents
  • Stakeholders added or removed
  • Workflow activity

To view record activity:

  1. In the left sidebar, click Inventory.

  2. Under the record type drop-down, select the type of record you want view activity for.

  3. Select a record or find your record by applying a filter or searching for it.

  4. In the expanded sidebar that appears for your record, click Activity.

Review model activity

  1. Select the name of your model you registered for this course to open up the record details page.
  2. In the left sidebar that appears for your model, click Activity.
  3. Confirm activity logged on the model reflects your activities from this training course.
  4. (Optional) Filter the activity by category or specific values to narrow down results.

When you’re done, click to continue.

Review workflows associated with your records or artifacts:

  • Review active workflows
  • Review workflow history

To review details for workflows currently active:

On records

  1. In the left sidebar, click Inventory.

  2. Select a record or find your record by applying a filter or searching for it.

  3. On the landing page of your record, locate the active workflows section.

  4. Click on the name of any active workflow to review that specific workflow’s details.

  5. Click on the tab you would like to review:

    • Details — Name and description (if present) of the workflow, the workflow’s status and progress (if applicable), when it was created, and when it was started.
    • Activity — History of updates to the workflow on that record, including notes submitted during transitions.
    • Artifacts — Artifacts for that record created within the duration of that workflow’s runtime.
    • Workflow — Overview of the entire workflow. The current workflow step is highlighted on this view.

On artifacts

  1. On the details page of your artifact, locate the active workflows section.

  2. Click on the name of any active workflow to review that specific workflow’s details.

  3. Click on the tab you would like to review:

    • Details — Name and description (if present) of the workflow, the workflow’s status and progress (if applicable), when it was created, and when it was started.
    • Activity — History of updates to the workflow on that artifact, including notes submitted during transitions.
    • Workflow — Overview of the entire workflow. The current workflow step is highlighted on this view.

Review timelines of workflows running or previously run:

On records

  1. In the left sidebar, click Inventory.

  2. Select a record or find your record by applying a filter or searching for it.

  3. On the record details page, click the Workflows tab to view a timeline of workflows associated with that record.

    If workflows are running or have previously run on your record, you can:

    • Click on any listed workflow to review the details of that specific workflow.

    • Filter the view using Filter to display only runs from specific types and statuses of workflows.

    • Click See All Workflows to see records of workflows by status:

      • Active — Workflows currently running.
      • Scheduled — Workflows slated to run in the future.
      • Waiting — Workflows in a Wait state.
      • Finished — Workflows that have reached an End state.
      • Aborted — Cancelled workflows.
      • Available — Workflows available to be run manually.

On artifacts

On the artifact details page, click the Workflows tab to view a timeline of workflows associated with that artifact.

If workflows are running or have previously run on your artifact, you can:

  • Click on any listed workflow to review the details of that specific workflow.

  • Filter the view using Filter to display only runs from specific statuses of workflows.

  • Click See All Workflows to see records of workflows by status:

    • Active — Workflows currently running.
    • Scheduled — Workflows slated to run in the future.
    • Waiting — Workflows in a Wait state.
    • Finished — Workflows that have reached an End state.
    • Aborted — Cancelled workflows.
    • Available — Workflows available to be run manually.

View active workflows

  1. Select the name of your model you registered for this course to open up the record details page.
  2. On the landing page of your model, locate the active workflows section.
  3. Click on the name of any active workflow to review that specific workflow’s details.
  4. Close the detail view for your workflow.
  5. Click See All Workflows to see records of workflows by status.

When you’re done, click to continue.

Work with analytics

Welcome to Analytics

Here, you can find executive summaries, track information on records (models), artifacts (findings), and more.

Default report widgets are provided in the Models (model-type records only) and Validation Issues (Validation Issue artifacts only) dashboards.

You can supplement these with your own custom reports in additional dashboards to suit your organizational needs.

When you’re done exploring, click to learn more about analytics.

Reports will not include data or artifacts from archived records.

  1. In the left sidebar, click Analytics.

  2. Click on the tab for the type of reports you want to review.

  3. For each of the bar or pie charts, hover for numerical breakdowns or click on individual sections to get a more detailed view.

    For example, to see all validation issues related to specific risk area:

    1. Click on the Validation Issues tab.
    2. Under Validation Issues by Risk Area, click on the risk area you want more details for.
    3. On the page that opens, the applicable validation issues are listed:
      • Filter this list of validation issues
      • Or customize column headers that display on this validation issue view
    4. To get more details on the validation issue itself, click on the title of the issue to take you to the Validation Issues section for the specific record the validation issue applies to.

View detailed analytics

  1. Click on the tab for the type of reports you want to review.
  2. For each of the bar or pie charts, hover for numerical breakdowns or click on individual sections to get a more detailed view.

Manage custom reports

Learn more …


Try it live on the next page.

To supplement the default reports with your own custom reports, first create a custom analytics page, then add your custom visualizations:

  • 1. Create an analytics page
  • 2. Create a visualization

  1. In the left sidebar, click Analytics.

  2. Click Add Page.

  3. On the Add New Page module, enter the following:

    • page name
    • description (optional)

  4. Click Add New Page to create your custom analytics page.

  1. Select Add Widget then Add Visualization.

  2. On the Add Visualization panel, enter your title.

  3. Select a visualization type:

    • Bar Chart
    • Pie Chart
    • Counter

  4. Select a dataset:

    • Artifact - Limitations — Logged Limitation type artifacts. only.
    • Artifact - Policy Exceptions — Logged Policy Exception type artifacts only.
    • Artifact - Validation Issues — Logged Validation Issue type artifacts only.
    • Inventory Records — Records logged in your inventory.

  5. Select the visualization configuration options to apply to the dataset.

  6. When you are done configuring your dataset, click Add Visualization to insert your custom widget onto the analytics page.

Create an analytics page

  1. Click Add Page.
  2. On the Add New Page module, enter the page name and the description.
  3. Click Add New Page to create your custom analytics page.

When you’re done, click to continue.

Create a custom visualization

  1. Click on the tab for the custom page you added previously.
  2. Click Edit Dashboard and select Add Widget then Add Visualization.
  3. On the Add Visualization panel, enter your title.
  4. Select a visualization type.
  5. Select a dataset.
  6. Select the visualization configuration options to apply to the dataset.
  7. Click Add Visualization to insert your custom widget onto the analytics page.

When you’re done, click to continue.
